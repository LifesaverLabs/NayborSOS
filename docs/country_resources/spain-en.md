## Cultural Considerations & Local Context

### Languages
- **Official language:** Spanish (Castilian)
- **Co-official languages:**
  - Catalan (Catalonia, Balearic Islands, Valencia)
  - Basque (Basque Country, part of Navarre)
  - Galician (Galicia)
  - Aranese (Aran Valley)
- **Emergency services:** 112 has interpreters available in multiple languages
- **Widely spoken languages:** English (especially in tourist cities), French, German

---

### Regional Differences

**Healthcare System:**
- Managed by autonomous communities
- Emergency numbers may vary (061, 112)
- Each region has its own protocols

**Urban vs Rural Areas:**
- Cities: Response time generally 8-15 minutes
- Rural areas: May be longer, especially in mountainous regions
- Islands: May require air evacuation for serious emergencies

**Regions with Special Considerations:**
- **Coasts:** Maritime rescue services (Salvamento Mar√≠timo - 900 202 202)
- **Mountains:** Civil Guard - Mountain Service (062)
- **Islands:** Coordination with insular services

---

### Common Emergency Types

- **Cardiac emergencies:** Leading cause of death
- **Traffic accidents:** High rate on roads
- **Heat-related emergencies:** Summer heat waves (especially in south)
- **Floods:** Risk in coastal areas and near rivers (DANA/cold drop)
- **Forest fires:** Summer, especially in Mediterranean
- **Drownings:** Coasts during tourist season

---

### Cultural Practices

- **Community solidarity:** Mediterranean culture of mutual aid
- **Social life:** Strong neighborhood culture in traditional areas
- **Schedules:** Be aware of siesta time (14:00-17:00) in some regions
- **Festivities:** During local festivals, emergency services are reinforced
- **Multilingualism:** Respect and use co-official languages in corresponding regions

---

### Legal Protections

**Good Samaritan Law:**
Spain has legal protection for those who provide assistance:

- **Article 195 of the Penal Code:** Legal obligation to help people in danger
- **Failure to provide assistance:** It is a crime NOT to help when you can do so without risk to yourself
- **Protection for those who help:** Those who act in good faith and according to their abilities are legally protected
- **Professional standards:** Healthcare professionals must act according to their training

**Key principle:** You are legally protected and often legally obligated to help in emergencies if you can do so safely.

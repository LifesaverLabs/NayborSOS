## **Executive Summary**
A federal program offering free Basic Life Support (BLS) training to all residents of the United States would cost between **$700 million and $2.6 billion per year**, depending on participation levels and training frequency. This estimate is based on conservative assumptions, realistic cost modeling, and operational approaches proven in Norway, Denmark, Japan, and Singapore.

At scale, this program would become one of the highest-ROI public-health investments in U.S. history, with potential downstream economic benefits of **$140–445+ billion per year** from saved lives and avoided disability.

# **2. Cost Per Trainee: Detailed Breakdown**
Modeled after real costs in U.S. fire departments, community health programs, and Scandinavian national CPR systems.

## **2.1 Instructor labor**
- 1 instructor per ~12 students  
- 3 hours per course (prep + teardown included)  
- Fully loaded government pay rate: **$40–$50/hr**

**Cost per trainee:**  
➡️ **$10–$12**

## **2.2 Training equipment and amortization**
### Manikins and AED trainers
- High-durability CPR manikin: $500–$1,200 each  
- AED trainer: $300–$600  
- Useful life: 5–8 years  
- Per-trainee amortization: **$2–$4**

### Maintenance and replacement
- Lung bags, valves, cleaning kits  
- **$1–$2 per trainee**

**Total equipment + amortization:**  
➡️ **$3–$6 per trainee**

## **2.3 Consumables**
- Barrier devices / pocket masks  
- Nitrile gloves  
- Sanitation wipes  
- Paperwork or digital certification administration

**Cost per trainee:**  
➡️ **$3–$5**

# **6. Cost-Efficiency Strategies That Bring Costs Down**

## **6.1 Embedding BLS in the public-school curriculum**
- Zero marginal facility cost  
- Captures every future adult automatically  
- International best practice (France, Nordics, UK)

## **6.2 Leveraging fire/EMS agencies**
- Existing training rooms  
- Existing instructor workforce  
- Already publicly funded

## **6.3 Integration with driver licensing**
- Dramatically increases uptake at low cost  
- Proven highly effective in Denmark and South Korea

## **6.4 Digital pre-learning**
- Videos + quizzes reduce in-person training time  
- Cuts instructor load by 30–40%

## **6.5 Partnering with national nonprofits**
- American Heart Association  
- Red Cross  
- National Bike & Scooter Safety coalitions  
- Veterans and National Guard units

Partnerships reduce the need for federal FTEs.
